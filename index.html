THE SIBERIAN TIGER

Siberian Tigers are the only tigers that live and survive in the snow. They are the largest cats in the World. They are the national animal of South Korea and an important symbol of their culture.

Scientific Name:Panthera tigris altaica

Average Size:10–12 ft (3–3.7 m) from head to tail

Average Weight:396–675 lbs (180–306 kg)

Average Lifespan:15–20 years in the wild, up to 25 years in captivity

Geographical Range:Previously throughout the Korean Peninsula, currently found in parts of Russia and China

Habitat:Taiga, open woodland, and mountainous regions

Conservation Status:Endangered (IUCN Red List)

Siberian tigers are incredibly solitary animals. They are extremely territorial and do not like to be around other tigers. Their territories are marked by spraying, and some of their territories can cover hundreds of square miles. Siberian tigers are also excellent swimmers, and they often traverse rivers and streams in search of prey such as deer and wild boar.
